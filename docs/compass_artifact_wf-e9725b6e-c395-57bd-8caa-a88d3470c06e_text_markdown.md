# Zero-to-B2 German Study Plan: A Complete Goethe-Aligned Reference (A1 → A2 → B1 → B2)

## TL;DR
- **Yes, you can go from A0 to Goethe-Zertifikat B2, and the most efficient route is to treat each CEFR level as a discrete "unit" built around three official Goethe documents per level — the free Wortliste (vocabulary), the Prüfungsziele/Testbeschreibung (objectives), and the free Modellsatz/Übungssatz (practice exam)**, all downloadable from goethe.de for A1, A2, and B1. The Goethe-Institut states that reaching B2 takes, depending on prior knowledge, "600 bis 800 Unterrichtsstunden à 45 Minuten" of cumulative study.
- **Vocabulary scope grows sharply per level:** ~650 words (A1) → ~1,300 lexical units (A2) → ~2,400 lexical units (B1) → an estimated 3,000–4,000+ at B2. Critically, the Goethe-Institut publishes **free official Wortlisten for A1, A2, and B1 only**; there is **no free standalone official B2 word list** — B2 vocabulary lives inside the paid handbook *Goethe-Zertifikat B2: Prüfungsziele, Testbeschreibung*.
- **Grammar and exam format escalate predictably:** A1/A2 are single (non-modular) exams scored out of 100 with a 60% pass; B1 and B2 are **modular** (four independently-passed modules, 60% each). Grammar moves from present tense + nominative/accusative (A1) → dative + Perfekt + subordinate clauses (A2) → Konjunktiv II + passive + relative clauses + Genitiv (B1) → Konjunktiv I, advanced passive, participial/nominal style (B2).

## Key Findings

1. **The single most efficient study architecture** is to anchor every level to its three free/official Goethe artifacts (Wortliste, Prüfungsziele, Modellsatz). For B2 the free Wortliste is the one gap — plan to buy the handbook or use a reputable commercial word list.
2. **Vocabulary counts (official Goethe figures):** A1 ≈ 650 entries — Goethe states "Als aktiver Wortschatz sollte einem Lernenden auf dieser Stufe etwa die Hälfte dieser circa 650 Einträge der Wortliste zur Verfügung stehen"; A2 ≈ 1,300 lexical units ("Der dem Goethe-Zertifikat A2 zugrunde liegende Wortschatz umfasst circa 1300 lexikalische Einheiten"); B1 ≈ 2,400 lexical units ("umfasst circa 2400 lexikalische Einheiten"). B2 has no official free list.
3. **A1 and A2 are single exams; B1 and B2 are modular** — a decisive practical difference: at B1/B2 you can sit, pass, and retake individual skills, which changes how you sequence exam attempts.
4. **Textbook backbone that works for self-study:** *Menschen* or *Schritte international Neu* (A1–B1), then *Aspekte neu* (B1+–C1) for B2, with *Grammatik aktiv* (A1–B1 and B2–C1) as the grammar reference and *Sicher!* as an alternative B1+/B2 course.
5. **Free supplementary media:** Deutsche Welle's "Nicos Weg" (A1–B1) video course is the strongest free structured resource for a beginner, plus DW's grammar and news-in-slow-German offerings.

### LEVEL A1 — Goethe-Zertifikat A1: Start Deutsch 1

**Vocabulary**
- **Official list:** *Goethe-Zertifikat A1: Start Deutsch 1 – Wortliste*. Scope: ~650 entries. Goethe states verbatim: "Als aktiver Wortschatz sollte einem Lernenden auf dieser Stufe etwa die Hälfte dieser circa 650 Einträge der Wortliste zur Verfügung stehen." The list contains thematic areas, "13 häufig gebrauchte Wortgruppen" (numbers, days, months, etc.), and an alphabetical list with example sentences.
- **Where to download (free, official):** `https://www.goethe.de/pro/relaunch/prf/de/A1_SD1_Wortliste_02.pdf`
- **Youth variant (Fit in Deutsch 1):** `https://www.goethe.de/pro/relaunch/prf/de/Goethe-Zertifikat_A1_Fit1_Wortliste.pdf`
- Note: the list is a reference/overview of the level's demands, not a teaching tool — Goethe explicitly says it is "less suitable" for drilling vocabulary and recommends a course plus didactic material.

**Grammar (to master actively)**
- Nouns: gender (der/die/das), plural forms, capitalization
- Articles: definite (der/die/das), indefinite (ein/eine), negative (kein)
- Personal pronouns (ich, du, er/sie/es, wir, ihr, sie/Sie); possessive articles (mein, dein, sein …)
- Present tense (Präsens): regular and irregular verbs; sein and haben
- Modal verbs (können, müssen, wollen, dürfen, sollen, mögen/möchten) — second verb to end as infinitive
- Separable verbs in present tense (prefix to end: aufstehen → ich stehe … auf)
- Cases: Nominative (subject) and Accusative (direct object), with article declension (der → den, ein → einen)
- Negation: nicht vs. kein and placement
- Sentence structure: subject–verb–object, verb-second (V2) in main clauses, yes/no questions and W-questions (wer, was, wo, wann, wie, woher, wohin)
- Prepositions (basic): in, auf, mit, bei, nach, an, für …
- Numbers, telling time, dates, days, months; simple conjunctions (und, oder, aber, denn)
- Imperative (basic); war (past of sein)

**Topics** (from the official Themen inventory / Sprechen tasks — ~12 everyday areas): Person (name, address, age, family status, nationality, appearance, daily routine); Wohnen (housing, rooms, furniture); Umwelt/weather; Reisen/Verkehr (travel, transport); Essen und Trinken; Einkaufen/Gebrauchsartikel; Dienstleistungen (post, bank, telephone, police); Erziehung/Ausbildung/Lernen; work/school; free time, hobbies, sport; clothing; body & health; appointments/arrangements.

**Exam format** (single, non-modular exam; conducted & graded uniformly worldwide):
- **Hören** (Listening): ~20 min, 3 parts — short everyday conversations, phone messages, public announcements
- **Lesen** (Reading): ~25 min — short notes, ads, signs, notices
- **Schreiben** (Writing): ~20 min — fill in a simple form + write a short personal text about an everyday situation
- **Sprechen** (Speaking): ~15 min, in a small group (usually paired), 3 parts — (1) introduce yourself, (2) ask/answer everyday questions with prompt cards on two themes (e.g., Einkaufen, Familie), (3) formulate a request and respond to one using picture cards
- **Scoring:** max 100 points = 75 (written: listening+reading+writing) + 25 (speaking); **pass = 60% (60 points)**, no separate per-section cut-off. (Some sources frame it as a 60-point scale where each skill = 15 points; the official pass threshold is 60%.)
- **Guided learning time to reach A1:** Goethe states "zwischen 80 und 200 Unterrichtseinheiten à 45 Minuten."

**Practice material (free, official):** A1 Modellsatz + Übungssatz with audio. Model set PDF: `https://www.goethe.de/pro/relaunch/prf/materialien/A1_sd1/sd_1_modellsatz.pdf`. Landing page for online/printable training: `https://www.goethe.de/de/spr/prf/ueb/pa1.html` (mirrored on country portals).

### LEVEL A2 — Goethe-Zertifikat A2

**Vocabulary**
- **Official list:** *Wortliste Goethe-Zertifikat A2* (PDF, ~410 KB). Scope: **~1,300 lexical units** (building on A1) — Goethe states "Der dem Goethe-Zertifikat A2 zugrunde liegende Wortschatz umfasst circa 1300 lexikalische Einheiten." It is an excerpt from *Goethe-Zertifikat A2, Prüfungsziele Testbeschreibung* (Hueber, 2015). It lists thematic areas, word groups, and an alphabetical list; learners are expected to command this vocabulary at least receptively.
- **Where to download (free, official):** `https://www.goethe.de/pro/relaunch/prf/de/Goethe-Zertifikat_A2_Wortliste.pdf` (English-portal path also exists: `/pro/relaunch/prf/en/Goethe-Zertifikat_A2_Wortliste.pdf`)
- A machine-readable cross-reference of the A2 list is hosted by DWDS at `https://www.dwds.de/lemma/wortschatz-goethe-zertifikat/A2` (downloadable in CSV/JSON — useful for building Anki decks).

**Grammar (adds to A1)**
- **Dative case** (articles, dative pronouns; verbs like helfen, danken, gefallen); Accusative vs. Dative choice
- **Two-way (Wechsel) prepositions** (in, an, auf, über, unter, vor, hinter, neben, zwischen) with the Akkusativ/Dativ rule; prepositions governing dative (mit, bei, seit, aus, nach, zu, von)
- **Perfekt** (present perfect): haben/sein + Partizip II; past participle formation
- **Präteritum** of sein/haben (war, hatte)
- Separable & inseparable verbs (aufstehen vs. bekommen); reflexive verbs (sich freuen, sich waschen)
- Comparatives & superlatives (größer, am größten); als/wie
- **Adjective endings** (declension — introduced across nominative/accusative/dative)
- **Subordinate clauses** with weil, dass, wenn, ob (verb to end); indirect questions
- Infinitive with zu; um…zu
- Modal verbs in Präteritum (konnte, musste …); imperative (full)
- Time expressions (seit, vor, ab, bis, während); future with werden (introductory)

**Topics:** personal information & background, education, family, daily routine, work & profession, shopping, food & drink, housing & living environment, travel & transport, health, free time/leisure, weather, appointments — all still concrete/everyday. Speaking themes are drawn from the immediate living environment.

**Exam format** (single, non-modular; adult version "Goethe-Zertifikat A2" and youth version "A2 Fit in Deutsch"):
- **Hören:** ~30 min, 4 parts — everyday conversations, radio interviews, phone messages, public announcements
- **Lesen:** ~30 min, 4 parts — short newspaper articles, emails, ads, notices
- **Schreiben:** ~30 min — write messages relating to your immediate environment
- **Sprechen:** ~15 min, paired, 3 parts — (1) ask/answer questions about yourself, (2) talk about your own life on a familiar topic, (3) plan/agree something together with your partner
- **Scoring:** max 100 = 75 written + 25 speaking; **pass = 60 points overall.** Multiple exam-prep sources report additional internal minimums (at least 45 of 75 written and 15 of 25 speaking) — verify current rules with your local test centre.
- **Total time** (excluding breaks): ~1 hr 45 min
- **Guided learning time A1→A2:** 200–350 lessons total per Goethe.

**Practice material (free, official):** A2 Modellsatz (Erwachsene & Jugendliche) + Übungssatz with audio. Practice-training landing page: `https://www.goethe.de/de/spr/prf/ueb/pa2.html`.

### LEVEL B1 — Goethe-Zertifikat B1 (jointly with ÖSD & University of Fribourg)

**Vocabulary**
- **Official list:** *Goethe-/ÖSD-Zertifikat B1 Wortliste*. Scope: **~2,400 lexical units** — Goethe/ÖSD state "Der dem Goethe-Zertifikat B1 zugrunde liegende Wortschatz umfasst circa 2400 lexikalische Einheiten, die Deutschlernende auf der Niveaustufe B1 kennen sollten." It contains word groups (abbreviations, anglicisms, countries/nationalities, colours, numbers, measures, etc.) plus a large alphabetical list with example sentences. Taken from *Zertifikat B1: Prüfungsziele, Testbeschreibung* (Hueber, ISBN 978-3-19-031868-1; © 2016 Goethe-Institut & ÖSD).
- **Where to download (free, official):** `https://www.goethe.de/pro/relaunch/prf/de/Goethe-Zertifikat_B1_Wortliste.pdf`, also linked from the official B1 practice page. A DWDS cross-reference is at `https://www.dwds.de/lemma/wortschatz-goethe-zertifikat/B1`. This is described by exam-prep specialists as a "must-study" resource.

**Grammar (adds to A2)**
- **Konjunktiv II** (hätte, wäre, könnte, würde + infinitive) — polite requests, wishes, hypotheticals, conditional sentences
- **Passive voice** (Vorgangspassiv with werden + Partizip II) in present, past, and perfect; **Zustandspassiv** (sein + Partizip II); passive with modal verbs
- **Relative clauses** with der/die/das in all cases (incl. dative/genitive, prepositional relatives)
- **Genitive case** (introduction): possession, genitive prepositions (wegen, trotz, während, statt)
- **Präteritum** (narrative past, broader verbs) and **Plusquamperfekt** (hatte/war + Partizip II) with nachdem
- Full range of subordinating conjunctions (obwohl, damit, bevor, nachdem, seit(dem), als vs. wenn vs. wann)
- Connectors: deshalb, trotzdem, sowohl…als auch, entweder…oder, je…desto
- Infinitive constructions: um…zu, ohne…zu, (an)statt…zu
- Indirect speech / reported speech (dass-clauses; basic Konjunktiv I awareness)
- Verbs with fixed prepositions and prepositional adverbs (darauf, worauf); adjective declension consolidation; participles as adjectives; n-declension

**Topics** (broader, some abstract): work & career, education & training, travel, health, environment, media & internet, technology, leisure & hobbies, shopping & consumption, housing, relationships, culture, and some social/current topics — the shift to "independent user."

**Exam format** (MODULAR — four independent modules, taken together or separately; each module certificate = ¼ of the full certificate):
- **Lesen:** 65 min, 5 parts, ~30 items — blog/forum posts, newspaper article, everyday-situation matching, opinions, instructions
- **Hören:** ~40 min, 4 parts, ~30 items — announcements, conversations, interviews, discussions
- **Schreiben:** 60 min, 3 tasks — (1) informal message/email, (2) opinion/forum post, (3) semi-formal message
- **Sprechen:** ~15 min, paired, ~15 min prep — (1) plan something together, (2) present a topic (Präsentation), (3) discuss/give feedback
- **Scoring:** each module scored out of **100 points; pass = 60 points (60%) per module.** Grades: satisfactory 60–69, good 70–79, very good 80–89, excellent 90–100. Failed modules can be retaken individually within the same calendar year.
- **Written total** ~165 min; oral ~15 min.
- **Significance:** B1 is the minimum level for German naturalization/citizenship and many residence/vocational-training purposes.

**Practice material (free, official):** B1-Modellsatz Erwachsene (PDF ~887 KB) with Hören audio (~37 min) and Sprechen example video (~13 min), plus a full B1-Übungssatz (PDF ~4 MB), and Jugendliche versions. Landing page: `https://www.goethe.de/de/spr/prf/ueb/pb1.html`. Model set PDF: `https://www.goethe.de/pro/relaunch/prf/materialien/B1/b1_modellsatz_erwachsene.pdf`. The B1 Modellsatz is especially valuable because it includes sample writing answers with scoring commentary.

### LEVEL B2 — Goethe-Zertifikat B2 (modular version, in force since 2019)

**Vocabulary**
- **There is NO free standalone official Goethe B2 Wortliste.** Unlike A1/A2/B1, the Goethe-Institut does not publish a free B2 word-list PDF on goethe.de. The B2 vocabulary ("Wortschatz und Strukturen") is contained only inside the **paid handbook**: *Goethe-Zertifikat B2: Prüfungsziele, Testbeschreibung* (Hueber Verlag; Herausgeber Zentrale Goethe-Institut München; **ISBN 978-3-19-061868-2**; 100 pages; © 2018 Hueber, 1. Auflage Mai 2018; list price €33,50 via shop.hueber.de). The handbook explicitly includes "eine Zusammenstellung des Wortschatzes und der Strukturen."
- **Caveat on third-party claims:** Several commercial sites (e.g., germanvibes.org, deutschesprachzertifikate.com.de, various Scribd/Quizlet uploads) advertise an "official B2 Wortliste of ~3,000–4,000 words." These are **third-party compilations, not an official free Goethe list** — treat the "official" framing skeptically. They can still be useful as thematic study lists.
- **Practical vocabulary target:** roughly 3,000–4,000+ words/fixed expressions cumulative, organized around abstract/argumentative themes (politics, society, environment, science, technology, work, media). Build from the B1 list upward using a course book's word lists + spaced repetition (Anki).

**Grammar (adds to B1 — consolidation + refinement rather than many new structures)**
- **Konjunktiv I** (indirect/reported speech) — recognition and use, as used in journalism
- **Konjunktiv II** fully (all modal verbs; irreale Bedingungssätze, irreale Wünsche, als ob/als wenn)
- **Advanced/complete passive**: passive in all tenses, passive with modals, passive alternatives (man, sich lassen + Infinitiv, sein + zu + Infinitiv)
- **Futur II** (future perfect) for assumptions
- **Participial constructions / extended attributes** (Partizipialattribute: "die im Jahr 2020 gegründete Firma")
- **Nominalization / nominal style** (Nominalstil ↔ Verbalstil) and complex noun phrases
- **Genitive** fully (genitive prepositions; formal vs. von)
- Complex connectors: einerseits…andererseits, zwar…aber, nicht nur…sondern auch, je…desto/umso; causal/concessive/consecutive/final adverbials (weil/da/denn/deshalb/so…dass, obwohl/trotzdem, damit vs. um…zu)
- Verbs with prepositions + prepositional objects (advanced); adjective/noun valency; word-order refinement (Mittelfeld, tekamolo)
- Subjective use of modal verbs (er soll … / sie will … gewesen sein)

**Topics** (abstract, opinion-driven; the Sprechen/Schreiben themes rotate but cluster around): society & politics (democracy, migration, social justice, media), work & career (job applications, further training, work models, workplace stress), environment & sustainability (conscious living/consumption, waste avoidance), science & technology, education & studying (choice of study, studying in a foreign language, all-day schools), health & nutrition (fitness, meat-heavy diets, vegetarian options), consumption & advertising, travel & mobility (car-free city centres), and culture & media (social media). You must present arguments, weigh pros/cons, and defend a position.

**Exam format** (MODULAR — four independent modules; each can be taken/retaken individually; offered on paper or digitally. Introduced "ab 01.01.2019 an ausgewählten Prüfungszentren, ab 01.08.2019 weltweit"):
- **Lesen:** 65 min, 5 parts, **30 items** — forum posts, newspaper/magazine articles, commentaries, instructions; identify key info, viewpoints, opinions, rules
- **Hören:** ~40 min, 4 parts, **30 items** — interviews, presentations, conversations, discussions, announcements at natural speed (Teil 1 heard once, 10 items; Teil 2 twice; Teil 3 once; Teil 4 a lecture heard twice, items 23–30)
- **Schreiben:** 75 min, 2 tasks — (1) express & justify your opinion in a forum post on a current social topic; (2) a formal message/email in a professional context ("Sie äußern und begründen schriftlich Ihre Meinung in einem Forumsbeitrag zu einem aktuellen gesellschaftlichen Thema. Außerdem schreiben Sie eine formelle Nachricht im beruflichen Kontext.")
- **Sprechen:** ~15 min paired (~10 min if individual), plus **15 min preparation** — (1) a structured ~4-min presentation/Vortrag on a chosen topic (with intro/body/conclusion), then answer questions; (2) a discussion exchanging pro/contra arguments and defending your position
- **Scoring:** **each module scored out of 100 points; pass = 60 points (60%) per module** ("In der Prüfung lassen sich maximal 100 Punkte pro Modul erreichen. Die Bestehensgrenze liegt bei 60 Punkten, also 60 Prozent"). Independent modular passing; failed modules retaken individually. Note: an older, non-modular pre-2019 B2 used a combined 100-point scheme — ignore legacy PDFs.
- **Written total** ~180 min; whole exam ~190 min including speaking.
- **Guided learning time to reach B2:** Goethe states "600 bis 800 Unterrichtsstunden à 45 Minuten" (cumulative from zero).

**Practice material (free, official):** B2-Modellsatz Erwachsene (PDF ~7 MB) and Jugendliche versions, with Hören audio and a Sprechen example video, plus an accessible online practice set. Landing/Übungsmaterialien page: `https://www.goethe.de/ins/de/de/prf/prf/gzb2/ue9.html`. Model set PDF: `https://www.goethe.de/pro/relaunch/prf/materialien/B2/b2_modellsatz_erwachsene.pdf`. Accessible interactive set: `https://bfu.goethe.de/b2_mod_2MX6/`. Official rules: Durchführungsbestimmungen B2 (`https://www.goethe.de/pro/relaunch/prf/de/Durchfuehrungsbestimmungen_B2.pdf`, Stand 1 Sept 2025).

### Cross-level resources (build your whole plan on these)

**Core textbook series (self-study friendly):**
- **A1–B1:** *Menschen* (Hueber) — the most widely used course worldwide, story-based with recurring characters; buy both Kursbuch + Arbeitsbuch. Alternatives: *Schritte international Neu* (Hueber) or *Netzwerk* (Klett).
- **B1+–B2 (and C1):** *Aspekte neu* (Klett) — designed for the B1→B2 transition and B2 certificate prep. Alternative: *Sicher!* (Hueber).
- **Grammar reference/workbook across levels:** *Grammatik aktiv A1–B1* and *Grammatik aktiv B2–C1* (Cornelsen); *Übungsgrammatik Deutsch als Fremdsprache* (Hueber) for B1/B2. English-language reference: *Hammer's German Grammar and Usage*.
- **Exam-specific prep books:** *Fit fürs Goethe-Zertifikat* series and *Mit Erfolg zum Goethe-Zertifikat* (Klett) / *Prüfungstraining* (Cornelsen) per level.

**Free digital resources:**
- **Deutsche Welle (DW)** — "Nicos Weg" (full A1–B1 video course, free), plus grammar explanations, vocabulary trainers, and "Langsam gesprochene Nachrichten" (slow news) for listening at B1–B2.
- **Goethe-Institut** — free practice exercises per level, "Deutsch für dich" community, and the Modellsatz/Übungssatz downloads above.
- **Anki** — spaced-repetition flashcards; import the official A1/A2/B1 Wortlisten (DWDS provides A2/B1 in CSV/JSON).
- **Podcasts/YouTube:** Easy German, Coffee Break German (A1–A2), Slow German (B1), plus authentic B2 media (Zeit, tagesschau) as you advance.

## Recommendations

**Stage 0 — Setup (Week 1):** Download the A1, A2, and B1 Wortlisten and all four Modellsätze now (links above) so you always have the target in view. Pick one core course book (*Menschen A1.1*) + one grammar workbook (*Grammatik aktiv A1–B1*). Install Anki and import the A1 list.

**Stage 1 — A1 (≈2–4 months, ~80–200 lessons):** Work through *Menschen A1* + DW "Nicos Weg A1". Drill the ~650-word list to ~50% active recall (Goethe's own target). In the final 3–4 weeks, do the A1 Modellsatz + Übungssatz under timed conditions. **Benchmark to advance:** consistently scoring ≥80% on practice sets and comfortable with present tense, Nom/Akk, modal & separable verbs. Sit the exam only if you need the certificate; otherwise proceed straight to A2.

**Stage 2 — A2 (≈3–4 months):** *Menschen A2* + "Nicos Weg A2". Master dative, Perfekt, and subordinate clauses (weil/dass/wenn) — these are the top stumbling blocks. Expand vocabulary to the ~1,300 cumulative target. **Benchmark:** ≥75% on the A2 Modellsatz, can narrate past events with Perfekt.

**Stage 3 — B1 (≈4–6 months):** Switch toward *Aspekte neu B1 plus* or finish *Menschen B1*. Prioritize Konjunktiv II, passive, relative clauses, and Genitiv. Grow to ~2,400 words. Because B1 is **modular**, you may sit modules individually — but for self-study efficiency, prepare all four together. Use the B1 Modellsatz's sample writing answers to calibrate your Schreiben. **Benchmark:** ≥60% on every module in the Modellsatz (remember each module must independently reach 60%).

**Stage 4 — B2 (≈5–8 months):** Move to *Aspekte neu B2* and/or *Sicher! B2*. Since there is no free official B2 word list, either buy the *Prüfungsziele B2* handbook (ISBN 978-3-19-061868-2 — it also gives you the definitive Wortschatz+Strukturen and scoring criteria) or build thematic lists from your course book. Read/listen to authentic media daily (news commentary, discussion podcasts). Drill the B2 Schreiben (forum post + formal email) and the Sprechen presentation structure (intro → 2–3 arguments with examples → position). **Benchmark:** ≥60% on each of the four B2 modules in the official Modellsatz, with a repeatable 4-minute presentation template.

**Decision thresholds that change the plan:**
- If you are on a visa/citizenship timeline, note **B1 is the citizenship threshold** — you may want to certify B1 formally even if your goal is B2.
- If you plateau at a level (practice-set scores stuck <60% for 3+ weeks), add a tutor or a Goethe/DW course rather than pushing to the next book.
- At B1 and B2, if you narrowly fail one module, use the modular retake rather than re-sitting everything.

## Caveats
- **Link durability:** goethe.de periodically restructures URLs. The `/pro/relaunch/prf/...` PDF paths and `/de/spr/prf/ueb/...` practice pages were live as gathered here, but if any 404s, start from the level's main exam page on your local Goethe-Institut portal (goethe.de → Deutschprüfungen → [level] → Übungsmaterialien/Prüfungstraining). Avoid third-party PDF mirrors (Scribd, Course Hero, pdfcoffee) for the official lists — they are often outdated and may host copyrighted material.
- **Word counts are official Goethe figures for A1 (~650), A2 (~1,300), and B1 (~2,400)**, quoted directly from the respective Wortlisten; the B2 figure (~3,000–4,000) is an estimate from commercial prep sites, not an official Goethe count, because Goethe publishes no free B2 list.
- **A2 internal scoring minimums** (45/75 written + 15/25 speaking) are reported by exam-prep sites; the universally confirmed rule is 60% overall. Confirm the current exact scheme with your test centre before booking.
- **Exam-format specifics can be updated by Goethe** (the B2 exam was overhauled in 2019; A1/A2 wording refreshed periodically). Timings given (e.g., A1 ~20/25/20 min) are typical current figures; always cross-check against the Modellsatz you download and the current Durchführungsbestimmungen.
- **Grammar-by-level breakdowns** synthesize the official Prüfungsziele frameworks with standard CEFR-aligned course progressions (Menschen/Aspekte, telc/Goethe prep guides). There is natural overlap at level boundaries — a structure "introduced" at one level is consolidated at the next, so treat the lists as cumulative, not mutually exclusive.
- **ID requirement:** Since 1 September 2025, all Goethe exam candidates (A1–C2) must present a valid passport as the sole accepted ID ("Seit dem 1. September 2025 müssen alle Kandidat*innen … einen gültigen Reisepass vorlegen. Andere Ausweisdokumente werden nicht mehr als ID akzeptiert") — factor this into registration.
